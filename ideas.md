# Ideas de Diseño — Profe Carol Astrophysics Academy

## Opción A — "NASA Scientific Premium"
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Scientific Brutalism meets Space Exploration Aesthetics
**Core Principles:**
- Deep space darkness as the canvas — every element floats in the void
- Data-driven visual language: grid overlays, coordinate markers, scientific notation
- Controlled color bursts: only nebula blues and stellar golds break the dark
- Typography as architecture: large display numbers and labels create structure

**Color Philosophy:** Near-black (#050A14) background with electric cyan (#00D4FF) accents for data/UI elements, warm gold (#FFB347) for highlights and CTAs, and muted slate (#8BA3C7) for secondary text. Feels like a mission control dashboard.

**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial layout — hero uses a split composition with large text on the left and a dramatic planet/nebula visual on the right. Sections alternate between full-bleed and contained grid layouts.

**Signature Elements:**
- Thin coordinate grid lines overlaid on hero sections
- Circular orbit rings as decorative dividers
- Monospaced data labels on cards (like instrument readouts)

**Interaction Philosophy:** Hover states reveal "data overlays" — cards expand to show more information like a scientific instrument panel.

**Animation:** Slow parallax star field, subtle orbit animations on planet icons, fade-in with slight upward drift on scroll.

**Typography System:** Space Grotesk (display/headers) + DM Mono (data/labels) + Source Serif 4 (body). Bold weight contrast between display and body.
</text>

## Opción B — "Cosmic Academy" ✅ ELEGIDA
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Premium Educational Editorial — National Geographic meets ESA/NASA
**Core Principles:**
- Dark cosmos as the stage — deep navy/black backgrounds with luminous accents
- Clean academic hierarchy: clear sections, generous whitespace, no visual noise
- Warm humanity within the cosmos: gold and amber tones for warmth and trust
- Scientific credibility through restraint: fewer elements, more impact

**Color Philosophy:** Deep space navy (#03071E to #0A1628) as background, electric blue (#4CC9F0) for interactive elements, warm amber/gold (#F4A261) for CTAs and highlights, pure white for primary text. The palette feels like looking at a star through a telescope — dark, focused, luminous.

**Layout Paradigm:** Vertical editorial scroll with full-bleed hero, then alternating asymmetric content blocks. Programs section uses a bold two-column split. Pricing uses a horizontal card row with the featured plan elevated.

**Signature Elements:**
- Subtle star particle field (CSS/canvas) in hero background
- Thin glowing lines connecting sections (like constellation paths)
- Planet/star icons with soft glow halos

**Interaction Philosophy:** Buttons have a soft cosmic glow on hover. Cards lift with a subtle shadow and border glow. Smooth, confident transitions — not playful, but alive.

**Animation:** Twinkling star particles in hero, smooth section reveals on scroll (framer-motion), gentle float animation on planet decorations.

**Typography System:** Outfit (headers — geometric, modern, clean) + Crimson Pro (body — academic, readable, elegant). NOT Inter. Headers are bold and large; body is comfortable at 17-18px.
</text>

## Opción C — "Explorer's Log"
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Retro-Futurist Science Journal
**Core Principles:**
- Aged paper textures meet digital space imagery
- Explorer's notebook aesthetic: hand-drawn constellations, margin notes
- Warm sepia tones contrasted with cold space photography
- Storytelling-first layout: every section feels like a chapter

**Color Philosophy:** Cream/parchment (#F5ECD7) for content areas, deep space black for full-bleed sections, rust orange (#C44B2A) as accent, and ink blue (#1A2744) for text. Feels like an explorer's journal from the future.

**Layout Paradigm:** Magazine-style with irregular column widths, pull quotes, and sidebar annotations. Hero is a full-bleed space photo with overlaid handwritten-style title.

**Signature Elements:**
- Dotted constellation lines connecting section headers
- Vintage star chart backgrounds in section dividers
- "Mission log" numbered entries for program descriptions

**Interaction Philosophy:** Hover reveals "annotations" — tooltip-style notes appear as if written in the margins.

**Animation:** Page-turn transitions between sections, ink-draw animations on constellation lines, typewriter effect on key statistics.

**Typography System:** Playfair Display (display headers) + Libre Baskerville (body) + Courier Prime (data/code elements). Classic editorial with space-age data.
</text>
